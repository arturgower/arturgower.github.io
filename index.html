Selected Publications

Learning about random media from near-surface backscattering: using machine learning to measure particle size and concentration

We ask what can be measured from a random media by using backscattered waves, emitted from and received at one source. We show that in 2D both the particle radius and concentration can be accurately measured for particles with Dirichlet boundary conditions. To answer these questions we use supervised machine learning together with a large, precise, data set of simulated backscattered waves.
A. L. Gower, R. M. Gower, J. Deakin, W. J. Parnell, I. D. Abrahams
arXiv, 2018.
Preprint Project

Reflection from a multi-species material and its transmitted effective wavenumber

We deduce closed-form expressions for the transmitted effective wavenumber of a material comprising multiple types of inclusions or particles (multi-species), dispersed in a uniform background medium. The expressions, derived here for the first time, are valid for moderate volume fractions and without restriction on the frequency. The average backscattering from cylindrical inclusions is also formally derived.
A. L. Gower, M. J. Smith, W. J. Parnell, I. D. Abrahams
Proceedings of the Royal Society A (accepted), 2018.
Preprint Project

A new restriction for initially stressed elastic solids

We introduce a fundamental restriction on the potential energy of solids under stress. The restriction is a consequence of energy conservation. Most potential energies used do not satisfy this restriction, and may therefore lead to unphysical behaviour, which we illustrate through a simple example. To remedy this shortcoming, we derive examples of potential energies that do satisfy the restriction. We also show that using initial strain (often from a virtual configuration) to model initial stress leads to potential energies that automatically satisfy the restriction.
A. L. Gower, T. Shearer, P. Ciarletta
QJMAM, 2017.
Preprint Project Paper

Initial stress symmetry and its applications in elasticity

How do we describe the elastic response of a stressed solid? We show that when the potential energy depends on the stress present in the solid, then a restriction we call Initial Stress Symmetry (ISS) has to be satisfied. We show how this symmetry can be useful in an example from biomechanics. We use ISS to calculate the optimal stress within an arterial wall and derive the optimal target residual stress.
A. L. Gower, P. Ciarletta, M. Destrade
Proceedings of the Royal Society A, 2015.
